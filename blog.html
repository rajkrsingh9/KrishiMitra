Water Movement in India: A Path Toward Sustainable Water Management

India is home to 18% of the world's population but has only 4% of its water resources. Since 2010, India has witnessed several transformative water movements aimed at tackling water scarcity, improving irrigation efficiency, and restoring traditional water bodies.

Key Government Initiatives:

  • 🌊 Jal Shakti Abhiyan – Focuses on groundwater recharge and large-scale conservation efforts.
  • 🌿 Atal Bhujal Yojana – Aims at sustainable groundwater management.
  • 🏞️ Namami Gange Programme – Works on pollution control and river restoration.

State-Level Programs:

  • 🏝️ Mission Kakatiya (Telangana) – Revived lakes and village reservoirs.
  • 💧 Jalyukt Shivar Abhiyan (Maharashtra) – Focused on water conservation.

The Neeranchal Watershed Program and emerging IoT-based smart water management systems are further strengthening sustainable water use through AI-driven analytics, precision irrigation, and real-time monitoring.

With climate change exacerbating water stress, platforms like KrishiMitra are playing a crucial role in integrating data-driven solutions to optimize water use, reduce wastage, and support sustainable farming practices across India.
