Introduction

Water scarcity is one of the most pressing challenges of our time. Agriculture, which is the backbone of most economies, relies heavily on water for irrigation. With the growing pressure on water resources, it becomes vital to understand how much water is available and how efficiently it is being used.

In many regions, surface and groundwater supplies are shrinking due to overuse, pollution, and climate change. This puts immense strain on food production and overall water sustainability. Without precise knowledge of water availability and consumption patterns, effective management becomes impossible.

Every Drop Counts: Tackling Water Scarcity for a Sustainable Future

Water scarcity is an escalating issue impacting communities, industries, and ecosystems worldwide. Agriculture, the largest consumer of freshwater, is at the heart of this challenge. Let’s delve into the key problems and their impacts.

Challenges and Impact on Agriculture

Overuse of groundwater and surface water threatens sustainability, worsened by a lack of regional data and awareness about water footprints. This leads to droughts, crop failures, and inefficient farming practices, reducing productivity and straining resources further.
